Why Collaborative Studying Matters for PEBC Success

Research consistently shows that collaborative learning enhances understanding, retention, and academic performance. For international pharmacy graduates preparing for the PEBC Evaluating Exam, studying with peers offers several critical advantages:

Enhanced Understanding Through Discussion

When you explain concepts to others or hear different perspectives, you deepen your own understanding. Discussing complex pharmacy topics like pharmacokinetics or Canadian pharmacy regulations with peers helps clarify misconceptions and reinforces learning.

Improved Retention Rates

Studies show that students retain 50-90% more information when they learn through discussion and teaching compared to passive reading. This is crucial for the PEBC EE, which tests comprehensive knowledge across multiple pharmacy domains.

Accountability and Motivation

Studying with friends creates a sense of accountability. When you commit to a study session with peers, you're more likely to follow through, stay focused, and maintain consistent study habits—essential for PEBC exam success.

Immediate Problem-Solving Support

When you encounter a challenging question or concept, having study partners means instant help. Instead of spending hours stuck on a single problem, you can quickly discuss it with peers and move forward.

However, traditional collaborative studying has limitations. Coordinating schedules, finding meeting spaces, and managing separate communication tools while trying to study from the same materials creates friction. This is where StudyTogether revolutionizes the experience.

How StudyTogether Works: Step-by-Step Guide

StudyTogether is designed to be intuitive and user-friendly. Here's how to use this revolutionary feature:

1

Create or Join a Study Session

From the passEE app, navigate to the StudyTogether feature. You can either create a new study session or join an existing one using a session code shared by a friend. Each session can accommodate up to four participants, creating an optimal group size for effective collaboration.

2

Enter the Call Room

Once you've joined a session, you'll enter a dedicated call room. This is where the magic happens—you can see who's in the session and start a voice call with just one tap. The call room is integrated directly into the study interface, so there's no need to switch apps.

3

Start Solving Questions Together

After connecting via voice, you and your study partners can select a quiz set from passEE's comprehensive question bank of 2,900+ PEBC EE practice questions. When you start a quiz, all participants see the same question at the same time, enabling real-time discussion and collaborative problem-solving.

4

Discuss and Learn Together

As questions appear, you can discuss them with your study group in real-time. Share your thought process, ask questions, explain concepts to each other, and work through challenging problems together. This collaborative approach helps everyone understand the material better.

5

Review Answers and Explanations

After everyone has attempted a question, you can review the correct answer and detailed explanation together. Discuss why certain answers are correct, understand common mistakes, and ensure everyone grasps the underlying concepts.

Pro Tip: Start with easier topics to build group confidence, then gradually move to more challenging areas. This approach helps establish effective communication patterns and ensures everyone feels comfortable contributing.

Best Practices for Using StudyTogether

To maximize the benefits of StudyTogether, follow these proven strategies:

Choose Your Study Partners Wisely

Select study partners who are committed, reliable, and have similar study goals. A group of 2-4 people works best—large enough for diverse perspectives but small enough for everyone to participate actively.

Establish a Regular Schedule

Consistency is key. Set regular study sessions (e.g., twice a week) and stick to them. This creates accountability and ensures steady progress toward your PEBC exam date.

Prepare Before Sessions

Review the topics you'll be covering beforehand. Coming prepared allows for more productive discussions and deeper learning during the session.

Create a Conducive Environment

Find a quiet space with good internet connection. Use headphones to minimize background noise and ensure clear communication. Test your microphone before sessions.

Encourage Active Participation

Everyone should contribute. Take turns explaining concepts, asking questions, and leading discussions. Active participation enhances learning for all group members.

Focus on Weak Areas

Use StudyTogether sessions to tackle challenging topics. If everyone struggles with a particular area, spend extra time discussing it and working through related practice questions.

Keep Sessions Focused

Set a time limit for each session (1-2 hours works well) and stick to it. Have a clear agenda and stay on topic to maximize productivity.

Take Notes During Discussions

Jot down key insights, common mistakes, and important points discussed during sessions. These notes become valuable study materials for later review.
