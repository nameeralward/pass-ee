Balancing Work and PEBC EE Preparation: A Guide for Working Pharmacists

April 1, 2025 11 min read By Dr. N. Patel, PharmD

Dr. N. Patel, PharmD is a Canadian-licensed pharmacist and exam coach with 10+ years of experience helping international pharmacy graduates succeed on the PEBC exams. Learn more.

Last updated: April 8, 2025

Preparing for the PEBC Evaluating Exam while working full-time is one of the biggest challenges facing international pharmacy graduates. With 70% of PEBC EE candidates working while studying, finding the right balance between professional responsibilities and exam preparation is crucial for success.

This comprehensive guide provides practical strategies, real-world schedules, and proven techniques to help working pharmacists effectively prepare for the PEBC EE without compromising their job performance or personal well-being.

In this article:

  • Unique Challenges for Working Candidates
  • Effective Time Management Strategies
  • Sample Daily and Weekly Schedules
  • Maximizing Workplace Learning
  • Weekend Study Planning
  • Stress Management and Self-Care
  • Technology Tools for Busy Professionals

Unique Challenges for Working Candidates

Common Obstacles and Their Solutions:

1. Limited Study Time

Challenge: Only 2-3 hours available daily for study

Solution: Maximize efficiency through focused, high-yield study sessions

2. Mental Fatigue

Challenge: Exhaustion after long work days

Solution: Strategic timing and energy management

3. Irregular Schedules

Challenge: Shift work and unpredictable hours

Solution: Flexible study plans and mobile resources

4. Work-Study Conflict

Challenge: Feeling guilty about studying during work or working during study time

Solution: Clear boundaries and integrated learning

Success Rate Analysis:

Working vs. Non-Working Candidates:

  • Working candidates (part-time): 39% pass rate
  • Working candidates (full-time): 34% pass rate
  • Non-working candidates: 42% pass rate
  • Working candidates with structured plan: 41% pass rate

Effective Time Management Strategies

The Working Professional's Study Framework:

1. Time Audit and Planning

Step 1: Track your current time usage for one week

Step 2: Identify 'dead time' opportunities (commute, breaks, waiting)

Step 3: Calculate realistic available study hours

Step 4: Create a sustainable weekly schedule

2. The 60-20-20 Rule

  • 60% High-energy time: Complex topics, practice questions
  • 20% Medium-energy time: Review, reading
  • 20% Low-energy time: Flashcards, audio content

3. Microlearning Approach

  • 15-minute sessions: Quick review or 5-10 practice questions
  • 30-minute sessions: Topic review or calculation practice
  • 60-minute sessions: Mock exams or in-depth study
  • 90-minute sessions: Comprehensive topic coverage

Sample Daily and Weekly Schedules

Schedule Templates for Different Work Patterns:

Full-Time Day Shift (Monday-Friday)

Weekday Schedule:
  • 6:00 AM - 7:00 AM: High-yield study (1 hour)
  • 7:00 AM - 8:00 AM: Morning routine, commute
  • 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM: Work (apply learning when possible)
  • 5:00 PM - 6:00 PM: Commute, decompress
  • 6:00 PM - 7:00 PM: Dinner, family time
  • 7:00 PM - 8:30 PM: Focused study session (1.5 hours)
  • 8:30 PM - 9:00 PM: Light review or audio content
  • 9:00 PM onwards: Personal time, rest

Daily Total: 2.5 hours focused study + 30 minutes light review

Shift Work Pattern

Day Shift Days:
  • Pre-shift: 30 minutes light review
  • Break times: 15-20 minutes microlearning
  • Post-shift: 1-2 hours intensive study
Off Days:
  • Morning: 2-3 hours intensive study
  • Afternoon: Break or light activities
  • Evening: 1-2 hours review and practice

Part-Time Work Schedule

Work Days:
  • Morning: 1-2 hours before work
  • Post-work: 2-3 hours focused study
Non-Work Days:
  • Morning: 3-4 hours intensive study
  • Afternoon: 2-3 hours practice questions
  • Evening: Review and planning

Weekly Study Distribution:

Day Focus Area Study Hours Activities
Monday Pharmacotherapy 2.5 hours New topic + practice questions
Tuesday Calculations 2 hours Practice problems + review
Wednesday Pharmacology 2.5 hours Mechanisms + applications
Thursday Canadian Practice 2 hours Laws, regulations, guidelines
Friday Review Day 2 hours Weak areas + light practice
Saturday Comprehensive 4-5 hours Mock exam + detailed review
Sunday Active Rest 1-2 hours Light review + planning

Weekly Total: 16-18 hours

Maximizing Workplace Learning

Integrating PEBC Preparation into Your Workday:

1. Clinical Application Opportunities

  • Patient Counseling: Practice explaining drug mechanisms and effects
  • Drug Information: Research questions thoroughly using primary sources
  • Medication Reviews: Apply pharmacotherapy principles
  • Calculation Practice: Double-check dosing calculations

2. Break Time Optimization

  • 15-minute breaks: Review flashcards or do 5-10 practice questions
  • Lunch hour: 30 minutes eating + 30 minutes focused study
  • Slow periods: Read pharmacy journals or guidelines
  • Commute time: Listen to pharmacy podcasts or audio content

3. Learning from Workplace Scenarios

  • Document interesting cases for later study
  • Ask supervising pharmacists about their reasoning
  • Connect real patient cases to PEBC topics
  • Practice explaining clinical decisions

4. Professional Development Alignment

  • Choose continuing education relevant to PEBC content
  • Attend pharmacy conferences and workshops
  • Join professional study groups
  • Subscribe to Canadian pharmacy publications

Weekend Study Planning

Making the Most of Your Days Off:

Saturday - Intensive Study Day

  • 8:00 AM - 9:00 AM: Light breakfast, review previous week
  • 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM: Mock exam or comprehensive practice (2 hours)
  • 11:00 AM - 11:30 AM: Break
  • 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM: Review mock exam, identify weak areas (1.5 hours)
  • 1:00 PM - 2:00 PM: Lunch break
  • 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM: Focused study on weak areas (2 hours)
  • 4:00 PM - 4:30 PM: Break
  • 4:30 PM - 5:30 PM: Practice questions and review (1 hour)
  • Evening: Personal time, relaxation

Total Study Time: 6.5 hours

Sunday - Review and Planning Day

  • 9:00 AM - 10:00 AM: Week recap and planning (1 hour)
  • 10:00 AM - 11:30 AM: Light topic review or reading (1.5 hours)
  • 11:30 AM - 12:00 PM: Plan upcoming week's study schedule
  • Afternoon: Personal activities, rest
  • Evening: Light review or audio content (30 minutes)

Total Study Time: 3 hours

Weekend Study Tips:

  • Start early: Take advantage of fresh morning energy
  • Take regular breaks: 15 minutes every 90 minutes
  • Vary study methods: Mix reading, practice questions, and review
  • Maintain balance: Reserve time for personal activities
  • Prepare for the week: Organize materials and plan ahead

Stress Management and Self-Care

Maintaining Physical and Mental Health:

Physical Health

  • Sleep: Maintain 7-8 hours nightly
  • Exercise: 20-30 minutes daily, even if just walking
  • Nutrition: Regular meals, limit caffeine after 2 PM
  • Hydration: 8-10 glasses of water daily

Mental Health

  • Meditation: 10 minutes daily mindfulness
  • Boundaries: Set clear work-study-personal time
  • Support system: Regular contact with family/friends
  • Professional help: Counseling if needed

Study-Related Stress

  • Realistic goals: Set achievable daily targets
  • Progress tracking: Celebrate small wins
  • Flexibility: Adjust plans when needed
  • Perspective: Remember the bigger picture

Work-Life Integration

  • Communication: Inform family of study schedule
  • Quality time: Plan dedicated family/personal activities
  • Efficiency: Minimize time wasters
  • Help: Accept assistance with household tasks

Warning Signs of Burnout:

  • Decreased job performance
  • Constant fatigue despite adequate sleep
  • Loss of motivation for studying
  • Increased irritability or mood changes
  • Physical symptoms (headaches, muscle tension)
  • Neglecting personal relationships

Action Plan: If experiencing multiple symptoms, take a 2-3 day study break and reassess your schedule.

Final Tips for Success

Mindset Strategies

  • View work experience as complementary to study
  • Focus on quality over quantity of study time
  • Embrace the challenge as career development
  • Remember that many successful candidates have balanced work and study

Practical Hacks

  • Prepare study materials the night before
  • Use voice memos to record key points during commute
  • Study the same topic at the same time daily
  • Keep backup study materials for unexpected free time

Long-term Success

  • Start preparation 6+ months before exam date
  • Build study habits gradually
  • Create multiple backup plans for disruptions
  • Maintain connections with other working candidates

Red Flags to Avoid

  • Sacrificing sleep consistently
  • Ignoring family/personal relationships
  • Letting work performance suffer
  • Cramming instead of consistent study
