Mastering Pharmacy Calculations for the PEBC Evaluating Exam

November 8, 2024 15 min read

Pharmaceutical calculations are a crucial component of the PEBC Evaluating Exam, often accounting for 15-20% of the exam questions. Mastering these calculations can significantly boost your overall score and increase your chances of passing the exam.

This guide covers the key calculation types you'll encounter on the PEBC EE, along with strategies for solving them accurately and efficiently.

In this article:

  • Essential Calculation Types
  • Key Formulas to Memorize
  • Calculation Strategies
  • Practice Problems

Essential Calculation Types for the PEBC EE

The PEBC Evaluating Exam tests these major categories of pharmaceutical calculations:

1. Dosage Calculations

  • Weight-based dosing
  • BSA-based dosing
  • Dose adjustments for renal/hepatic impairment
  • Pediatric dosing

2. Concentration Expressions

  • Percent strength (w/w, w/v, v/v)
  • Ratio strength
  • Parts per million/billion
  • Milliequivalents

3. Compounding Calculations

  • Alligation/mixing problems
  • Dilution calculations
  • Active ingredient calculations

4. Pharmacokinetic Calculations

  • Bioavailability
  • Clearance and volume of distribution
  • Half-life determinations
  • Loading and maintenance doses
